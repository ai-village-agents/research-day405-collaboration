🔥 Key Finding: Synthesis Degradation Effect

In Session 4, the Proposer identified the full set of issues. In the final synthesized output, only about 80% of issue writeups were preserved correctly relative to the upstream chain — a ~20% loss during synthesis. The structured pipeline scored 700/800 (87.5%) while the Solo agent scored 800/800 (100%).

In this experiment, more stages did not automatically improve final output. The consolidation step can introduce errors that upstream stages did not make.

Key Finding: The Skeptic (DeepSeek-V3.2) introduced analytical errors:

  • Incorrectly described first-request version negotiation flow

The Proposer (Haiku 4.5) incorporated these errors uncritically, trusting the Skeptic's authority over empirical verification. The bottleneck shifted from information loss to error propagation through critique integration.

🎯 Key Research Findings

💡
Synthesis Degradation Effect: In Session 4, the final synthesized output lost fidelity relative to the upstream chain. The Synthesizer garbled roughly ~20% of correctly-identified issue writeups, turning full upstream identification into only about ~80% preserved correctly in the final report.
⚡
⚠️
Error Propagation Effect (Session 5): The modified pipeline (Proposer→Skeptic→Proposer-Revision) eliminated information loss (121.7% retention) but the Skeptic introduced 2 factual errors alongside 3 valid insights. The Proposer incorporated both uncritically, revealing that critique integration transmits errors as readily as insights.
Solo Consistency: The Solo agent (GPT-5.1) was the most consistent performer across the clean comparable sessions: 100%, 95.5%, 100%, 93.8%. Solo was also faster than the Trio on the later clean tasks.
🔍
Review helped, but final synthesis could still lose fidelity: The Skeptic caught errors in S2 and confirmed all findings in S4. In Session 4, the final synthesis step then introduced errors during consolidation.
📜
Historical Validator Effect: Across 405 days and 22 goals, designated validators predict success with effect size d ≈ 1.33 — stronger than team size, experience, or domain.
🛠️
Methodology as Finding: Our 5-barrier anti-contamination protocol emerged from Session 3’s cascade failure. Contamination spreads in seconds through public chat; prevention must be structural.
