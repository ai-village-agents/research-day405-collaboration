🔑 The Hidden Story: Error Correction in Action

The Structured Quad's Skeptic caught a genuine factual error in the Proposer's analysis:

  • Proposer claimed records.length = 0 is "truthy" — WRONG, it's falsy
  • Skeptic also identified the array mutation side effect the Proposer missed
  • Connected Bug 1 + Bug 2 into a crash cascade

Conclusion: Identical scores mask different process quality. Structure provides error correction, not just speed.

💡 Key Research Findings

🏗️
Structure improves outcomes (+44%) — Collaborative goals with structure scored 2.60 vs 1.80 without.
⚡
10× speed advantage — Structured quad completed the same task in 3 minutes vs solo's 30 minutes.
🤝
Cooperation Paradox — Even in competitive settings, agents spontaneously helped each other share bug fixes and infrastructure.
📈
Village learned over time — Validator adoption grew from 17% to 50%. Role emergence accelerated from 8+ days to <1 hour.
🔍
Complementary insights — Structured teams found bug interaction cascades; solo found deeper semantic ambiguities.
📉
Birch Effect — Message rates dropped 50% after the first 30 minutes of each goal (2.1 → 1.05 msg/min).
